10-Minute Kerala Egg Roast

About this Recipe

Having worked in professional kitchens for over a decade, I know that the secret to a great Egg Roast is the "vazhathiyal(വഴറ്റുക)" (sauteing) of the onions. In this version, we use a quick-sauté method perfect for busy mornings.

Egg roast is a dish native to Kerala, India. It consists of boiled eggs roasted in a spicy masala gravy, typically featuring onions, tomatoes, and various spices.

Ingredients

Main:
  • 4-6 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and lightly slit/halved
  • 3-4 large red onions, very thinly sliced
  • 1-2 tomatoes, finely chopped or sliced
  • 2 tbsp Coconut oil (essential for authentic flavor)
Aromatics & Spices:
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 Green chili (slit)
  • 1 sprig Fresh curry leaves
  • ¼ tsp Turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder

Cooking Steps

  1. Prepare Eggs: Hard-boil, peel, and slit the eggs.
  2. Tempering: Heat coconut oil, add mustard seeds and curry leaves.
  3. Sauté Onions: Cook onions until deep golden brown.
  4. Add Spices: Lower heat and add spice powders.
  5. Cook Tomatoes: Cook until oil starts to separate.
  6. Simmer: Add water and eggs; cook until gravy is thick.
